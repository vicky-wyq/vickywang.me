User research

Opportunity and use cases

Problem / why design it

Business owners want to know the engagement and sentiment of people. Reddit is the platform where people comes to discuss product and brands.

For example, a ccompany wants to know how people feel about a new feature launched, or a new item released. They also want to understand the positive and negative experiences with the brand and products. understand the reason based on a bunch of data.

Goal

My goal is to design a keywords analysis tool with sentiment score for Reddit.

This app will help the business owner understand how their customer feels about their brand.

Also help the customer support team is informed in real-time about any negative comments. The keywords on Reddit get detected extremely quickly.

Why Reddit

Redditors are less welcoming of promoted content, topic and content are about honesty and transparency. People like to read and share Reddit thoughts before or after purchase.

The business owner wants to understand how Reddit users respond to their brand, they would like to monitor changes in how user perceive their brand and products.

Problem with search keywords on Google or Reddit

Reddit search can not provide overall counts, for example, if you are looking for a specific post, search on google or Reddit will find the result easily, but will not have overall metrics for keywords.

Reddit API

Reddit API allows quarrying feeds containing posts.

About sentiment

Sentiments can be generated through machine learning.

Without a sentiment, score businesses can not quantify the sentiment and compare it across different dimensions.

Businesses not only be able to understand if user sentiment towards their brand/product is positive or negative, but would also like to quantify this sentiment so that it can be compared. For example, compare one date to another date, one keyword to another, one community to another community

Usability testing

Tasks

Users should be able to add and delete the keywords

Users should be able to see how popular the keywords in Reddit posts were and what was the sentiment behind them.

Users should be able to filter data by a certain date range or a single date

Users should be able to click the link to see the original Reddit posts. For example, short the table with negative sentiment or most popular posts, open the most popular link to see the details

Feedback from the testing

It is a bit ambiguous to navigate from keywords to the dashboard. each keyword has its own dashboard. Do I still need to have the dashboard tag on the left navigation panel? Or only have a list of keywords, move the setting to the top right.

When comparing data, the user has to open two windows to compare, maybe there is a better way to compare the data side by side
