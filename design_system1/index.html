Main goal

The objective of this design system is to effectively organize all the components on the website, making them easy to locate and reuse. It also aims to categorize different types of elements, allowing for easy comparison of their visual effects.

Breakdown

  • Establish a responsive grid as the basis
  • Select a typography and color palette, and make adjustments based on user feedback and other relevant factors
  • Design and implement components, testing their behavior, and updating them as needed to ensure they work seamlessly within the system
  • Providing a usage demonstration helps to clarify the underlying logic of a system, making it easier to compare and improve upon

Moderated tree testing

Research plan & testing questions

Background

The goal is to add a new group prayer feature to the Glorify app, simplifying navigation. Currently, accessing this section is less intuitive than desired, so my plan is to enable users to easily find and engage with group prayers within the content.

Research goal

To evaluate and optimize the navigation structure of the Glorify app, ensuring that users can intuitively and efficiently locate the group prayer feature within the content.

Research Method

Moderated testing

Participants

3

Research questions

  • Find a Group Prayer: "Please locate the section where you would join a group prayer. How would you navigate there?"
  • Understanding the Path: "Can you describe the path you took to reach the group prayer section? Was there anything confusing or unclear?"
  • Alternative Routes: "Is there another way you might have found the group prayer section? Why did you choose the path you took?"
  • Ease of Use: "On a scale from 1 to 5, how easy or difficult was it to find the group prayer feature? What, if anything, would you change to make it easier?"
  • General Feedback: "Do you have any general comments or suggestions regarding the navigation to the group prayer section, or anything else within the app?"

Wireframe test 2

Test plan - Wireframe Test on Figma

Define the test plan

Test goal

Concentrate on the Group Prayer flow, which includes joining existing Christian group worship sessions and establishing a group prayer within the wireframe.

Method

Moderated tests

Expected outcome

Successfully join an existing Christian group worship and create a new one with a smooth and user-friendly process

Scenarios & tasks

Scenarios

You are a new user of the Glorify app with a keen interest in Christian group worship. You want to participate in an existing worship group, and also wish to create a new group to share your unique perspectives.

Tasks - Participate group prayer

  • Open the Figma file containing the wireframes for the Glorify app.
  • Navigate to the 'Group Worship' section within the app.
  • Browse the available Christian group worships.
  • Evaluate the information provided about each group (meeting times, the focus of the group, size of the group, etc.)
  • Join the group that you find most appealing based on the information provided.

Tasks - Create a new group prayer

  • Open the Figma file containing the wireframes for the Glorify app.
  • Go to the 'Group Worship' section and select 'Create a new group'.
  • Fill out the necessary information to set up your new group, including name, description, etc.
  • Complete the process and create the new group.

Measurement & Potential roadblocks

Success Metrics

  • User Understanding and Flow Comprehension: Assess whether users understand the overall concept and can navigate through the wireframe without confusion. Are the flow and steps intuitive?
  • Task Success Rate: Identify how many users are able to successfully complete key tasks within the wireframe (e.g., creating a group prayer, joining one, etc.). Any struggle may indicate that the design needs refinement.
  • Time on Task: Measure the time taken for users to complete specific tasks. This can provide insights into the complexity or simplicity of the wireframe.
  • Error Rate: Monitor the number of errors or misunderstandings users make while interacting with the wireframe. Errors can help identify areas for improvement.
  • User Satisfaction and Feedback: Collect qualitative feedback from users regarding their satisfaction with the design, aesthetics, and overall concept. This can be done through interviews or questionnaires.
  • Clicks to Complete a Task: Analyzing the number of clicks needed to complete a task may indicate the efficiency and user-friendliness of the design.

Potential roadblocks and solutions

  • Roadblocks: The wireframes may not clearly represent the intended engaging group prayer experience.
  • Solution: Use detailed annotations and visual cues in the wireframes to show interactive
  • Roadblocks: Users might desire to chat and interact with each other before the group prayer commences. However, currently, there is no chat feature available during the waiting time.
  • Solution: Highlight the chat feature prominently and make it accessible from various parts of the app. Provide clear instructions or prompts for starting a conversation prior to the group prayer.

Enhancement

Outcomes

While this product is just a concept, there are still takeaways!

What went well so far:

User Engagement:

Test results have indicated that users are highly engaged with the group prayer feature and particularly appreciate the interactive elements during group playing.

Ease of Navigation:

Users found the navigation to the group prayer section intuitive, and the time taken to reach this section was within acceptable limits.

Ease of Joining Existing Group Prayers:

The process for joining existing group prayers received positive feedback, indicating the feature is very easy to use

What could be improved:

Streamlining Group Prayer:

Refine the process for creating and join a new group prayer. This could involve simplifying certain steps or providing clearer instructions to enhance the user experience.

Detailed Guidance:

While users found the general navigation intuitive, some indicated that more detailed guidance could be provided for creating new group prayers. Providing clearer instructions or tooltips might help improve this.

Enhancing Interactive Features During Waiting Time:

While interactive features were appreciated, they could be expanded to provide a more engaging and enriching experience to users during the waiting period.

Enhancing Search & Invite Functionality:

Develop a search function and incorporate an invitation feature for group prayers, enabling users to discover appropriate group prayer sessions and invite friends to pray together.

Learnings

Qualitative versus Quantitative Data:

During this study, we relied heavily on qualitative data gathered through interviews and tests due to time constraints. While this provided valuable insights into the user experience and perceptions, it did limit our ability to draw wide-reaching, statistically significant conclusions. Moving forward, incorporating more quantitative data through surveys or larger-scale usability tests would provide a more holistic view of the user experience and help us to validate the insights gained from the qualitative data.

Understanding the Concept of Community:

Given the time constraints, we defined community as a "shared faith, mutual understanding, support, and the opportunity to connect with others." This was a suitable starting point for our study. However, the concept of community, especially within a religious context, is multifaceted and richly layered. If we had more time, it would have been beneficial to delve deeper into understanding the nuances of the Christian community from a social, psychological, and cultural perspective. This deeper understanding could inform more empathetic and holistic design decisions.
