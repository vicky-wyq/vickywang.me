PROBLEMS

Gardeners Hub Application aims to build the local market network and improve the horticultural experience by offering more comprehensive local market networking opportunities to the gardeners that result in reducing wasting resources and preventing establishment failure by improving learning and sharing experience. An overwhelming of resources and misunderstanding that result in failure horticultural experience by those involved in the horticultural process.

SOLUTIONS

Gardeners' Hub provides a local marketplace platform to help gardeners reduce wasting resources and a regional social network with the local community.

PROCESS

The goal of this project is to find solutions and ideas to help gardeners to improve their horticultural experience with neighbors and reduce wasting resources by selling or sharing locally.

BACKGROUND RESEARCH

Growing vegetables and house plants are more popular recently. Instead of shopping at the grocery store or shopping mall is more convenient to have house plants and fresh vegetables at home during this pandemic. Base on the necessity, this project focuses on developing a local marketplace platform that helps gardens share plants/tools and horticulture experiences. The goal is to help all gardeners get a good horticulture experience by engaging in horticulture events locally.

User Research

Competitive Analysis

USER RESEARCH

to understand the problems of the goal and context of use of potential users I used some blockbusting activity and applied different technicals to better analyze the problem from different perspectives. I began by interviewing three people with 6 months, 18 months, and 36 months of horticultural experience.

Gardeners’ pain points

· What is the major problem to plant their own food?

· How they maintain the garden and use what method?

Pain points the gardeners mentioned. The primary takeaways from the gardeners were as follows:

· Gardeners want to successfully get gardening information such as watering and seeding based on the local climate

· Gardeners feel frustrated when advice is not helping them to solve the problems of the location or conditions of their garden.

· Gardeners struggle to remember the complete tasks date.

· New gardeners often made mistakes to hurt their plants such as overwatering or over prune based on the online instructions

· There are overwhelming variables to grow plants for new gardeners

CONTEXTUAL INQUIRIES

I conducted four contextual inquiries by accompanying our target users on their site with 6 months, 12 months, and more than 18 months of horticultural experience. By conducting the contextual inquiries, I wanted to know more about:


Method

· How to identify problem?

· how to find information?

· did all information work?

Pain Points

· What are the major pain points

· how long do you spend gardening / day?

Wishlist

· What kind of service are they using or they wish a horticulture website to have.

Key Findings from the Contextual Inquiries

Finding 1

Gardeners want a variety of plants and horticultural tools. They would like to find friends nearby sharing plants and tools

“I always grow more seeds than I actually need for some reason.”

“After the seed sprout, Um, they grow much bigger than I thought, spacing between plants are requiaed”

“I bought many seeds, but still want to have more variety of them on my back yard”

“I have many pots, I do not really use all of them, but I won't throw them away.”


Finding 2

New gardeners often made mistakes to hurt their plants. based on the overwhelming information from the internet to made poor judgment.

““I start to grow some vegetable and indoor plants during covid pandemic, When their leaf turning yellow, I am always struggled about what to do. Um, especially, not sure about overwhater or under water. ”


Finding 3

Gardeners become frustrated when advice is not applicable to the location or conditions of their garden.(local climate)

“I bought a money tree from Home Depot, when the leaf turning yellow, I thought I did not watering it enough based on the image and article from the internet, eventually, it died by overwatering.”


Finding 4

Gardeners struggle to remember the date of the complete task.

“Adding watering records on a app and follow the app watering schedule was not always helpful. I wish I have an app providing a watering history. because my area has high humidity, when I follow the the watering schedule the app provided, I had killed several my favorate plants.”
