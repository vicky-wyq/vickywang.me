Context

Glorify is a mobile application that offers a vast library of content designed to support users in theirspiritual journey, particularly those who follow the Christian faith. The app provides a range of resources such as daily devotionals, scripture readings, prayer guides, and other faith-based content that users can access at their convenience. Glorify's content is designed to be immersive and engaging, with a focus on helping users deepen their spiritual connection and growth.

Opportunity

Despite its rich content, Glorify has been primarily a single-player experience, lacking the community engagement features that are integral to Christianity. This can limit the app's ability to fully meet the user's needs for social and collaborative experiences in their faith journey. To improve the user experience, I exploring ways to incorporate community engagement features that align with the values of Christianity.

Research

The research goal is to study the Christian community, understand their interaction patterns, discover why they wish to connect with others in the community, and explore how to incorporate this initial community version into the existing Glorify app.

Understand

Context & problem statement

Context

Glorify primarily offers a single-player experience , where users can explore a vast library of content in a feature known as the Daily Worship. Renowned as the top Christian Daily Worship app.

Problem statement

Glorify exists in a primarily isolated experience. Community is integral to Christianity. There’s an opportunity to fully meet the user's needs for social and collaborative experiences in their faith journey.

Research plan

Background

The Glorify platform has mainly been utilized as a single-player experience, which has made it challenging for users to establish new connections. In order to address this issue, my aim is to leverage the platform's existing infrastructure and introduce activities that promote connections and cater to the initial version of its community.

Group worship constitutes a significant aspect of Christian community activities. While Glorify offers a vast library of content for individual daily worship, this research will specifically concentrate on the communal aspects of worship.

Research goal

Understand both the successful methods and pain points of having group worship

Research Method

Semi-structured user interview

Research questions

  • How do Christians typically attend group worship? what they do? which group worship they like the most?
  • Why Christians participate in group worship? How did they initiate? what is their motivation?
  • What types of group worship have been most/least valuable to the participants in the past?

Participants

Christians/non-Christian seeking opportunities to connect and engage with fellow believers.

James, 38 years old, Software engineer, male, based in SF Bay Area

I engage in daily group worship by participating in morning prayer gatherings, small group Bible studies, and occasional attend traditional liturgical worship services in a Protestant church..

I most frequently visit small group Bible studies. I appreciate the in-depth discussions and the opportunity to connect with others on a deeper level in our shared exploration of the Bible. It is the setting where I find the most personal growth and fulfillment in my faith journey.

In a community of believers, we come together in group worship to experience a deep sense of connection and belonging. I find solace and strength in this collective gathering. Concept Test | Feedback session script

Research Goal

Gather feedback to understand users' initial impressions of the concepts.

Intro

Thanks for supporting me with this feedback session! I will present two early design concepts for a mobile app. The goal of these apps is to facilitate individuals in joining group worship, while also enabling interaction and connection with other fellowships within the Glorify app. I want to gather feedback about these ideas before I move forward with the design process.

Concept A

the first concept is adding a group prayer feature to the existing Christian single-player app. The goal is to help users discover like-minded individuals during group prayer sessions.

With the app, users can:

  • Create a group prayer room
  • Discover group prayers that align with their interests
  • Find a suitable group prayer to participate in
  • Engage in prayer with other fellowships within the room
  • Foster connections with other fellowships after the group prayer session

Concept B

This concept involves integrating a Bible study session into the existing Christian single-player app, aiming to facilitate meaningful connections among users who share similar beliefs and interests through these study sessions.

With the app, users can:

  • Create a Bible study room
  • Discover topics that align with their interests
  • Find a Bible study session to join
  • Engage in discussions with other fellowships within the room
  • Foster connections with other fellowships after the Bible study session

Conclusion

That's the end of our session! Thank you for providing your thoughts on these concepts. I appreciate your time.

Iteration Ideas

Feedback - Concept A

  • The concept is an extension of the existing prayer feature in the app.
  • In addition to praying together, users may also wish to communicate while they are waiting.
  • Perhaps there could be an alternative method for users to interact with other fellowships without needing to send a formal fellowship request.

Feedback - Concept B

  • It becomes quite complex when bible study is restricted to keyboard-based chatting only.
  • Discussing only one topic may become monotonous.
  • Spending too much time in a room can lead to losing focus on the topic.

I'd like to iterate on Concept A (Group prayer) by adding some elements from Concept B (Chatting before group prayer start).

  • In the new iteration, users inside the group prayer room have the ability to chat with each other before the group prayer session begins.
  • The app will retain the history of group prayers, including the fellowships who participated in the prayer sessions together.

Design

Strive to ensure that the initial version of the community design can be seamlessly integrated into the existing mobile app. Additionally, identify potential issues related to the concept and user flow.

Title

Group Prayer, Adding Fellowship, and Chatting - Wireframe Test on Figma

Test goal

focus on the overall functionality, focusing on group prayer, adding a new fellowship, and chatting with other fellowships.

Method

Moderated tests

Scenarios

You are a new user of the Glorify app with a keen interest in group prayers and fellowship. You want to participate in group prayers, add new fellowships, and chat with members of different fellowships.

Expected outcome

Successfully participate in a group prayer, add a new fellowship, and engage in a chat with other members of the fellowship with an intuitive and user-friendly process.

Tasks - Participate group prayer

  • Open the Figma file containing the wireframes for the Glorify app.
  • Navigate to the wireframe representing the 'Group Prayer' section within the app.
  • Participate in an ongoing group prayer by following along with the prayers and adding your own prayers as prompted by the app.

Tasks - Create a new group prayer

  • Open the Figma file containing the wireframes for the Glorify app.
  • Navigate to the wireframe representing the 'Group Prayer' section within the app.
  • Create a new group prayer

Tasks - Send a fellowship request

  • Explore available fellowships after group prayer
  • Add a fellowship that aligns with your interests.
  • Use the chat feature to start a conversation with members of the newly joined fellowship.

Tasks - Accept a fellowship request

  • Review a fellowship request including the fellowship profile and message
  • Accept the request
  • Use the chat feature to reply message

Success Metrics

  • Time taken to navigate to the 'Group Worship' section.
  • Time taken to understand the list of available groups.
  • Time taken to join an existing group.
  • Time taken to create a new group.
  • Satisfaction with both the joining and creation process (to be rated on a survey after completing the tasks).

Potential roadblocks and solutions

  • Roadblocks: The user might have difficulties finding the 'Group Worship' section
  • Solution: Make navigation within the app clear and intuitive.
  • Roadblocks: Information about the groups might be insufficient for the user to make a decision
  • Solution:Ensure there is enough information about each group.
  • Roadblocks: The process of creating a new group might be complex or unintuitive
  • Solution: Keep the group creation process straightforward and guided, with clear instructions at each step.

Analyze the testing results

Understanding the processes:

The majority of testers were able to understand the process of participating in group prayer, adding a new fellowship, and starting a chat within 2 minutes. They appreciated the clear annotations and visual cues.

Navigating the wireframes:

Testers generally found the wireframes easy to navigate, with a median time of 20 seconds to locate the 'Group Prayer' and 'Fellowship' sections. Some testers suggested that a table of contents or an overview map of the wireframes would be helpful.

Interactivity in group prayer:

Testers praised the representation of interactive elements in the group prayer wireframe. Some recommended more detailed annotations to fully express the real-time nature of reactions and comments during the group prayer.

Understanding the processes:

The majority of testers were able to understand the process of participating in group prayer, adding a new fellowship, and starting a chat within 2 minutes. They appreciated the clear annotations and visual cues.

Potential roadblocks and solutions

Creating a Group Prayer: Testers found the wireframes representing the creation/joining process of a group prayer quite intuitive. The median time to understand the process was 1 minute and 45 seconds. A few testers suggested providing more interactive features during waiting time. Some testers suggested having an option to save or bookmark specific group prayers for easy access in the future.

Joining an Existing Group Prayer:The process of joining an existing group prayer was found to be intuitive by most testers, with a median comprehension time of 1 minute and 30 seconds. Some testers suggested the addition of a search function or filter options to streamline the process of finding appropriate groups.

Adding New Fellowships after Group Prayer:The procedure of exploring and adding new fellowships post group prayer was clear to most testers, with a median time of 1 minute to understand the process. A few testers recommended highlighting the fellowship addition feature more prominently to ensure users are aware of this function.

Chatting with Fellowship Members Post-Prayer:The chat function was well-received with testers finding it easy to understand from the wireframes. The median time to comprehend the process was 1 minute and 15 seconds. Some testers suggested adding an indication of 'read' messages or 'typing' status for a more interactive chat experience.

Potential roadblocks and solutions

Interactive Features During Waiting Time:It seems users would appreciate more engagement while waiting for the group prayer to start. One solution could be to introduce interactive elements such as trivia questions about the Bible, thought-provoking quotes, or serene religious imagery. This could keep users engaged and occupied during the waiting time.

Joining an Existing Group Prayer:To facilitate the process of finding and joining suitable groups, introduce a search function or filtering options. These could include filters for group size, frequency of meetings, focus of prayers, or even a filter for groups that users have bookmarked or saved.

Adding New Fellowships after Group Prayer::To ensure users are aware of the ability to add new fellowships after a group prayer, make the "Add Fellowship" button more prominent. This could be done through changing its color, size, or location on the screen, or through an occasional prompt or notification suggesting that users explore new fellowships.

Chatting with Fellowship Members Post-Prayer::To enhance the chat experience, incorporate 'read' indicators for messages and 'typing' status for active conversations. These features could be represented in the wireframes with clear annotations. Also, consider adding push notifications for new chat messages to ensure that users don't miss out on ongoing conversations.

Iterate

The iteration prioritizes simplifying the user flow, making it intuitive to locate and navigate. Simultaneously, the design should be appealing enough to motivate users to participate in group activities and forge connections.

Title

Joining and Creating a Christian Group Worship on the Glorify App

Test goal

focus on the Group prayer, joining an existing Christian group worship and creating a new one within the Glorify app.

Method

Moderated tests

Scenarios

You are a new user of the Glorify app with a keen interest in Christian group worship. You want to participate in an existing worship group, and also wish to create a new group to share your unique perspectives.

Expected outcome

Successfully join an existing Christian group worship and create a new one with a smooth and user-friendly process

Tasks - Participate group prayer

  • Open the Figma file containing the wireframes for the Glorify app.
  • Navigate to the 'Group Worship' section within the app.
  • Browse the available Christian group worships.
  • Evaluate the information provided about each group (meeting times, the focus of the group, size of the group, etc.)
  • Join the group that you find most appealing based on the information provided.

Tasks - Create a new group prayer

  • Open the Figma file containing the wireframes for the Glorify app.
  • Go to the 'Group Worship' section and select 'Create a new group'.
  • Fill out the necessary information to set up your new group, including name, description, etc.
  • Complete the process and create the new group.

Success Metrics

  • Time taken to navigate to the 'Group Worship' section.
  • Time taken to understand the list of available groups.
  • Time taken to join an existing group.
  • Time taken to create a new group.
  • Satisfaction with both the joining and creation process (to be rated on a survey after completing the tasks).

Potential roadblocks and solutions

  • Roadblocks: The wireframes may not clearly represent the intended engaging group prayer experience.
  • Solution: Use detailed annotations and visual cues in the wireframes to show interactive
  • Roadblocks: Users might desire to chat and interact with each other before the group prayer commences. However, currently, there is no chat feature available during the waiting time.
  • Solution: Highlight the chat feature prominently and make it accessible from various parts of the app. Provide clear instructions or prompts for starting a conversation prior to the group prayer.

Analyze the testing results

Understanding the processes:

Most testers grasped the process of participating in group prayer and creating group prayers in less than 1 minute.

Navigating the wireframes:

Users easily found the 'Group Worship' section, taking just 15 seconds on average, which is significantly faster than before.

Group prayer experience:

The majority of users rated the group prayer experience as highly engaging, attributing the interactive elements to enhancing their overall enjoyment. However, a few users suggested that providing guidelines for participation could further improve the experience. Additionally, some individuals expressed a desire for even more interactivity to focus their praying experience.

Joining a group:

Users reported high satisfaction with the process of joining a group, indicating that the provided information about each group was comprehensive and helpful. The median time for this process was 30 seconds.

Creating a new group:

There were mixed reviews about creating a new group. Some users found the process straightforward, while others mentioned they would appreciate more guidance or clearer prompts. The median time to create a new group was 1 minutes.

Problems Identified:

Group prayer experience:Some testers expressed a desire for even more interactivity to focus their praying experience.

Proposed Solutions:

Enhance group prayer experience:To enhance the group prayer experience, create a high-fidelity prototype that prioritizes and amplifies engagement, focus, and interest during the group prayer sessions.

Joining and Creating a Christian Group prayer on the Glorify App - Wireframe Test on Figma

Test goal

focus on the Group prayer experience. Emphasize the group prayer experience and enhance user interaction

Method

Moderated tests

Scenarios

As a UX tester, you are examining a high-fidelity prototype of the Glorify app on Figma. Your goal is to explore the app's user experience, focusing on the process of creating or joining a group prayer and assessing the overall group prayer experience.

Expected outcome

Successfully understand and evaluate the user flow for creating or joining a group prayer and assess the overall group prayer experience, focusing on user interaction and engagement.

Tasks

  • Open the Figma file containing the hi-fi prototype for the Glorify app.
  • Navigate to the section of the prototype related to 'Group Prayer.'
  • Evaluate the process of creating a new group prayer and make note of any areas of improvement.
  • Explore the process of joining an existing group prayer and provide feedback on its intuitiveness and ease of use.
  • Evaluate the overall group prayer experience, focusing on user interaction, engaging elements, and overall design.
  • Participate in the group prayer and assess the interactive features during the waiting time.

Success Metrics

  • Time taken to navigate through the prototype to the 'Group Prayer' section.
  • Ease of understanding the process of creating/joining a group prayer (rated on a scale of 1-5).
  • Satisfaction with the interactive elements and overall design of the group prayer experience (rated on a scale of 1-5).

Potential roadblocks and solutions

  • Roadblocks: Roadblock: Prototypes may lack sufficient detail or interactive elements, leading to an inability to fully understand the intended user experience.
  • Solution: Include enough interactive elements in the prototype to allow testers to gain a full understanding of the user flow and intended interactions.

Analyze the testing results

Navigation to Group Prayer Section:

Most testers could navigate to the 'Group Prayer' section of the app relatively quickly. The average time taken was around 40 seconds, indicating that the path to this feature is clear and intuitive.

Group prayer experience:

The majority of users rated the group prayer experience as highly engaging, attributing the interactive elements to enhancing their overall enjoyment. However, a few users suggested that providing guidelines for participation could further improve the experience. Additionally, some individuals expressed a desire for even more interactivity to focus their praying experience.

Joining an Existing Group Prayer:

Joining an existing group prayer was generally well-received by testers, with an average ease-of-use rating of 4.5 out of 5. Some testers suggested introducing a search function to make it easier to find suitable group prayers.

Creating a New Group Prayer:

The process for creating a new group prayer was considered straightforward by most testers, with an average ease-of-use rating of 4.3 out of 5. However, a couple of testers felt that some steps could be better explained or streamlined.

Interactive Features During Waiting Time:

The interactive features during the waiting time were received positively, with testers appreciating the additional engagement. Some suggested that these features could be expanded to provide more variety or depth, such as more diverse trivia questions or additional quotes or readings.

Problems Identified:

Group prayer experience:Some testers expressed a desire for even more interactivity to focus their praying experience.

Proposed Solutions:

Enhance group prayer experience:To enhance the group prayer experience, create a high-fidelity prototype that prioritizes and amplifies engagement, focus, and interest during the group prayer sessions.

Outcomes

While this product is just a concept, there are still takeaways!

What went well so far:

User Engagement:

Test results have indicated that users are highly engaged with the group prayer feature and particularly appreciate the interactive elements during group playing.

Ease of Navigation:

Users found the navigation to the group prayer section intuitive, and the time taken to reach this section was within acceptable limits.

Ease of Joining Existing Group Prayers:

The process for joining existing group prayers received positive feedback, indicating the feature is very easy to use

What could be improved:

Streamlining Group Prayer:

Refine the process for creating and join a new group prayer. This could involve simplifying certain steps or providing clearer instructions to enhance the user experience.

Detailed Guidance:

While users found the general navigation intuitive, some indicated that more detailed guidance could be provided for creating new group prayers. Providing clearer instructions or tooltips might help improve this.

Enhancing Interactive Features During Waiting Time:

While interactive features were appreciated, they could be expanded to provide a more engaging and enriching experience to users during the waiting period.

Enhancing Search & Invite Functionality:

Develop a search function and incorporate an invitation feature for group prayers, enabling users to discover appropriate group prayer sessions and invite friends to pray together.

Learnings

Qualitative versus Quantitative Data:

During this study, we relied heavily on qualitative data gathered through interviews and tests due to time constraints. While this provided valuable insights into the user experience and perceptions, it did limit our ability to draw wide-reaching, statistically significant conclusions. Moving forward, incorporating more quantitative data through surveys or larger-scale usability tests would provide a more holistic view of the user experience and help us to validate the insights gained from the qualitative data.

Understanding the Concept of Community:

Given the time constraints, we defined community as a "shared faith, mutual understanding, support, and the opportunity to connect with others." This was a suitable starting point for our study. However, the concept of community, especially within a religious context, is multifaceted and richly layered. If we had more time, it would have been beneficial to delve deeper into understanding the nuances of the Christian community from a social, psychological, and cultural perspective. This deeper understanding could inform more empathetic and holistic design decisions.
